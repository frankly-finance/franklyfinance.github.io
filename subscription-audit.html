How to run this audit properly
01
Open your bank or credit card statement for the last 30 days. Find every recurring charge — monthly, annual, weekly, anything. Don't go from memory. Go from the statement. You will find something you forgot about.
02
Enter the monthly cost for every subscription you find. For annual subscriptions, divide by 12 and enter that. The annual column calculates automatically.
03
For each one, ask a single question: if this subscription didn't exist and I had to sign up again today, would I? If the answer is no, or "I'm not sure," mark it for cancellation. Uncertainty counts as a no.
04
Check the "forgotten" list above. These are the charges that don't fit neatly into any category but quietly keep running. At least one of them will apply to you.
05
Cancel the ones marked for cancellation before you close this tab. Not tomorrow. Now. Most services make cancellation deliberately inconvenient — expect a retention offer. Decline it unless it's meaningfully cheaper for something you genuinely use.
06
For subscriptions you're keeping, check whether a lower tier covers everything you actually use. Streaming, cloud storage, and software tools frequently default you to the mid-tier plan on signup. You probably don't need the features you're paying for.
07
Set a calendar reminder to repeat this in 12 months. New subscriptions accumulate. They always do.

"The goal isn't zero subscriptions. The goal is no subscriptions you've forgotten about, no subscriptions you're keeping out of inertia, and no subscriptions you'd be embarrassed to admit you're still paying for. That's a different standard than 'I use it sometimes.' Be honest with yourself. Your bank statement already knows."
